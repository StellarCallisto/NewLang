Verbs exist in two types, Solar and Lunar. These forms are conjugated a bit differently, and can be determined by their endings. They are conjugated by type and by the noun class that the subject is in (Animate, Inanimate, or abstract). Solar verbs ending in a consonant get the suffix appended, meaning that the whole verb is the stem.
